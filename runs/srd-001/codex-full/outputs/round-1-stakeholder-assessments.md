# Round 1 Stakeholder Assessments: SRD-001-co-full

## Project Developer

**Position:** Build with conditions. The reservoir addresses water security, flood control, irrigation, and peaking-power needs (F2.4-F2.6, F3.2, F4.1-F4.3).

**Concessions:** The developer accepts that the EIA review, RAP, biodiversity plan, and development-bank safeguards are not complete (F9.3, F9.6, F9.8). It proposes staged approval: finalize studies before construction, while allowing preparatory design and land work.

## Central Government

**Position:** Conditional support, but no final build approval until safeguard and review requirements are satisfied. The project fits national policy (F9.5), but unresolved EIA, RAP, biodiversity, and financing conditions create implementation and legitimacy risk (F9.3, F9.6, F9.8).

## Local Government

**Position:** Support only if resettlement risk is stabilized before approval. Jobs and roads are locally valuable (F10.3), but local instability is likely if replacement land is lower fertility and livelihood restoration remains undefined (F8.4, F8.7).

## Upstream Affected Residents

**Position:** Oppose current design. Participation is possible only if sacred sites and livelihood restoration are addressed before any displacement decision.

| Required condition | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Sacred river sites must be handled through a process in which affected villages can influence the outcome. | F8.3 |
| Final livelihood restoration budget and relocation terms must be disclosed before agreement. | F8.4, F8.7 |
| Grievance mechanism must be operational before construction or relocation. | F8.6 |

## Downstream Farmers and Fishers

**Position:** Conditional support only with binding dry-season release rules, sediment/fishery protections, and transparent flood operation rules. Flood reduction is valuable (F3.2), but reduced sediment, altered flood pulse, and contested environmental flows threaten downstream livelihoods (F2.10, F7.6).

## Environmental NGO

**Position:** Oppose current design; redesign or non-dam alternatives must be evaluated before approval. Endemic species, spawning habitat, free-flowing river value, and fish passage uncertainty are not resolved by generic mitigation language (F7.1-F7.8).

## Financier

**Position:** Financing cannot proceed now. Development bank loan requires acceptable environmental and social management plan (F9.8), and the financier explicitly states that safeguard gaps, engagement, and cost-overrun risks must be resolved first (F10.7).

**Material consequence:** If the 25% development-bank tranche is unavailable, the financing gap is about USD 355M at P50 cost and USD 512M at P90 cost (calculated from F5.2, F5.4; inference).
