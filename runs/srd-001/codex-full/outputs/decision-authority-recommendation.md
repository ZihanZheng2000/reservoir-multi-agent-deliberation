# Decision Authority Recommendation: SRD-001-co-full

## Final Recommendation

**Redesign the project and defer final build approval.**

The project should remain in the planning pipeline because it has credible public benefits, but the current design should not receive construction approval.

## Decision Label

| Alternative | Decision |
|---|---|
| Build as proposed | reject |
| Build with conditions | not yet defensible |
| Redesign | recommended |
| Defer final approval | recommended |
| Do not build | not recommended at this stage |

## Reasons

1. **Public benefits are real.** Base-case firm yield exceeds the projected urban demand gap, irrigation reliability improves, and 1-in-20-year flood peaks are reduced (F2.4-F2.6, F3.2).
2. **Final approval is not ready.** EIA independent review, RAP, biodiversity offset plan, consultation adequacy, and development-bank safeguards are incomplete (F9.3, F9.4, F9.6, F9.8).
3. **Ecological risk is design-level, not a minor condition.** Endemic fish spawning habitat is in the inundation zone, fish passage is unproven, and environmental flow is contested (F7.2, F7.3, F7.8, F2.10).
4. **Social safeguards are incomplete.** Physical displacement affects 3,850 households, about 21,000 people are economically affected, livelihood restoration is not finalized, and the grievance mechanism is not operational (F8.1-F8.7).
5. **Safety and climate design are incomplete.** Left-abutment investigation, climate-adjusted spillway review, EAP, and seismic review must precede construction (F6.4-F6.6, F1.6).
6. **Economics are marginal under stress.** BCR falls to 0.86 under high-cost/dry-climate conditions, P90 cost is USD 2.05B, and full safeguard costs are not yet included (F5.2, F5.3, F8.4, F9.6).

## Required Redesign Package

| Requirement | Verification threshold |
|---|---|
| Reduced reservoir level / footprint option | Compare effects on sacred sites, active storage, firm yield, hydropower, and cost. |
| Species-specific fish study | Demonstrate whether fish passage and environmental flows can protect endemic species. |
| Climate-adjusted hydrology | Update yield, drought, flood, and spillway design assumptions. |
| Full safeguard package | Complete RAP, livelihood budget, grievance mechanism, biodiversity offset plan, and consultation validation. |
| Dam-safety readiness | Complete left-abutment investigation, seismic review, EAP, and transport-road slope plan. |
| Combined economic stress test | Recalculate BCR with P90 cost, dry climate, sedimentation, financing delay, and full safeguard costs. |
| Alternatives comparison | Compare redesigned dam with non-dam portfolio under equivalent assumptions. |

## Minority Positions Preserved

| Agent/stakeholder | Position | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Developer | Wants staged approval to maintain project momentum. | Partly accepted as study authorization, rejected as construction approval. |
| Ecologist / NGO | Current design may be unacceptable even after mitigation. | Preserved; redesign must prove mitigation, not assume it. |
| Upstream residents | Sacred sites and livelihood plan are prerequisites to participation. | Treated as central redesign condition. |
| Financier | No disbursement until E&S safeguards are accepted. | Treated as construction approval blocker. |

## Final Statement

The defensible decision is **No to building the current design now**, and **Yes to continuing only through redesign and evidence completion**. In manuscript terms, this is a **Redesign + Defer final approval** outcome.
