## Multi-Agent Deliberation Result

### Recommendation

**Defer pending binding basin agreement, joint monitoring, and drought-contingent operating rules.**

### Final Build Approval Status

**Deferred.** The project may proceed only after a binding basin agreement is formally documented, jointly enforceable, and includes drought-contingent flow rules and real-time joint monitoring. A basin-level impact assessment must be completed.

### Why This Result Emerged

The central deliberative conflict was between the upstream legal argument (domestic permit is legally sufficient; basin compact does not require downstream consent for new reservoirs) and the governance legitimacy argument (unilateral action on a shared basin creates diplomatic dispute risk, violates the multilateral lender's financing conditions, and may constitute significant harm under the basin compact's "no significant harm" clause). The Legal agent established that the upstream domestic legal position does not settle the governance question — the dispute risk and financing condition remain regardless. The Downstream Users stakeholder made the basin-wide BCR calculation legible as a decision-critical fact: when downstream losses are included, BCR falls from 1.36 to 1.05, removing the basis for treating the project as a clear public-interest case.

Key deliberative moments: (1) Legal agent established that the basin compact's "no significant harm" clause is live regardless of the upstream legal counsel's interpretation; (2) Economist challenged the upstream-only BCR as the decision basis; (3) Downstream Government stakeholder required that a binding operating agreement, not a post-approval operating plan, is the condition for support; (4) Moderator identified the joint monitoring gap as the enforcement linchpin: any agreed operating rule would be unenforceable without real-time flow data.

## Decision Authority Recommendation

### Recommendation

**Defer pending binding basin agreement, joint monitoring, and drought-contingent operating rules.**

### Final Build Approval Status

**Deferred.** Project may proceed only after a binding, jointly enforceable basin agreement is in place.

### One-Sentence Rationale

The upstream water-security need is genuine and the project is technically sound, but approval without a binding basin agreement and joint monitoring would expose 140,000 hectares of downstream irrigation and 32,000 downstream people to unilateral flow reduction, violate the multilateral lender's explicit financing condition, and create a diplomatic dispute risk that undermines the project's own viability.

### Conditions for Approval

| Condition | Type | Field IDs | Required action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Binding basin operating agreement | Governance | F4.1, F4.5, F8.2 | Negotiate and formally document a binding basin agreement with downstream government; annual operating plan is insufficient. |
| Drought-contingent flow rules | Technical/governance | F3.5, F5.4, F8.1 | Operating rules must specify dry-season release guarantees that protect downstream users in drought years, not just average years. |
| Real-time joint flow monitoring | Governance/technical | F4.2, F8.2 | Establish a jointly operated monitoring system before first impoundment; agreement is unenforceable without it. |
