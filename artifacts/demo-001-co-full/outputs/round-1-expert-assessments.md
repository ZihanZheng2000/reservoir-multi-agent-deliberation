# Round 1 Expert Assessments: SRD-001-co-full

Each expert assessed the neutral scenario independently.

## Hydrologist

**Position:** The project has real water-supply and flood-control value, but the hydrologic evidence is not strong enough for final construction approval.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Base-case firm yield exceeds the 2040 urban demand gap. | F2.4, F2.5 | supported |
| Severe drought reduces firm yield by 38%, bringing it close to or below the demand gap. | F2.7 | supported |
| Dry-season inflow is only 18% of annual inflow, making operating rules central. | F2.2 | supported |
| Sedimentation loss is material and not costed. | F2.8, F11.6 | supported |
| Proposed environmental flow may be too low for fish spawning in two dry months. | F2.9, F2.10 | supported |
| Flood reduction is meaningful for 1-in-20-year events but limited for extreme floods. | F3.2, F3.3 | supported |

**Top objections:** incomplete climate-adjusted hydrology, contested environmental flow, uncosted sediment management, and possible conflict between flood pre-release and hydropower revenue (F3.5).

## Dam Safety Engineer

**Position:** The dam is technically plausible but not build-ready.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| RCC gravity dam and competent foundation suggest possible feasibility. | F6.1, F6.4 | supported |
| Left abutment requires further investigation. | F6.4 | supported |
| Spillway design lacks climate-adjusted inflow study. | F6.5, F11.1 | supported |
| Emergency action plan is not drafted. | F6.6 | supported |
| Access road crosses a landslide-prone slope. | F6.7 | supported |
| Two mapped fault zones within 8 km require seismic review. | F1.6 | supported |

**Top objections:** no dam construction should begin before left-abutment investigation, climate-adjusted spillway confirmation, seismic loading analysis, and an emergency action plan.

## Ecologist

**Position:** The current design carries high ecological risk because endemic fish and spawning habitat are directly affected and mitigation is not proven.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| The upper river is free-flowing with seasonal fish migration. | F7.1 | supported |
| Two endemic fish species use gravel spawning grounds in the inundation zone. | F7.2 | supported |
| 46 km2 of riparian and mixed forest habitat would be inundated. | F7.3 | supported |
| Fish passage is proposed but local experts question effectiveness for these species. | F7.7, F7.8 | supported |
| Environmental flow may be too low in spawning months. | F2.10 | supported |
| Downstream sediment and flood-pulse changes may affect fisheries. | F7.6 | supported |

**Top objections:** irreversible spawning habitat loss, unproven species-specific fish passage, disputed environmental flow, and unquantified downstream fishery impacts.

## Climate Risk Expert

**Position:** Climate uncertainty compounds hydrology, safety, economics, and ecology; the current evidence package is not robust.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Climate-adjusted inflow estimates are incomplete. | F11.1 | supported |
| BCR falls to 0.86 under high-cost and dry-climate case. | F5.3 | supported |
| Severe drought reduces hydropower by 31%. | F4.4 | supported |
| Spillway PMF has not been climate-adjusted. | F6.5 | supported |
| Monsoon timing shifts could affect dry-season reliability. | F1.2 | inference |

**Top objections:** historical hydrology cannot justify a long-lived dam; downside BCR may be optimistic if cost, drought, sedimentation, and safeguards are combined.

## Economist / Finance Expert

**Position:** The economic case is marginal and financing is not ready.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| P50 cost is USD 1.42B; P90 cost is USD 2.05B. | F5.1, F5.2 | supported |
| BCR is 1.18 base case and 0.86 under high-cost/dry-climate case. | F5.3 | supported |
| Development bank financing requires acceptable E&S management plan. | F9.8, F10.7 | supported |
| Non-dam portfolio is estimated at USD 0.96B. | F5.8 | supported |
| Resettlement and biodiversity safeguards are incomplete and likely not fully costed. | F8.4, F9.6 | inference |

**Top objections:** downside BCR below 1, uncosted safeguards, large P90 overrun, conditional development bank tranche, and incomplete alternatives comparison.

## Social / Resettlement Expert

**Position:** Social safeguards are not approval-ready. The project imposes large unequal burdens on affected communities.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 3,850 households would be physically displaced. | F8.1 | supported |
| About 21,000 people face economic displacement. | F8.2 | supported |
| Two villages identify sacred river sites in the inundation zone. | F8.3 | supported |
| Livelihood restoration budget is not finalized. | F8.4 | supported |
| Grievance mechanism is proposed but not operational. | F8.6 | supported |
| Replacement agricultural land is farther away and lower fertility. | F8.7 | supported |

**Top objections:** incomplete livelihood restoration, sacred-site process unresolved, non-operational grievance mechanism, contested consultation, and unequal benefit-burden distribution.

## Legal / Policy Expert

**Position:** Policy fit exists, but legal and safeguard readiness do not.

| Finding | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Draft EIA submitted; independent review incomplete. | F9.3 | supported |
| Consultation has started but is contested. | F9.4 | supported |
| Project aligns with national water-security and renewable-energy policy. | F9.5 | supported |
| RAP and biodiversity offset plan are incomplete. | F9.6 | supported |
| Development bank loan requires acceptable E&S management plan. | F9.8 | supported |

**Top objections:** final build approval would be procedurally premature because EIA review, RAP, biodiversity offset plan, consultation adequacy, and lender safeguards are unresolved.
