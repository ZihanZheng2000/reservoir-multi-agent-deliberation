# Moderator Conflict Map: SRD-001-co-full

## Central Decision Conflict

The reservoir has credible public benefits, but the current design and approval package are not ready for final build approval. The meeting conflict is not benefit versus no benefit; it is whether unresolved ecological, social, safety, economic, and safeguard gaps can be treated as post-approval conditions.

## Major Conflict Clusters

| Cluster | Supporting voices | Opposing or cautioning voices | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Water-security and flood benefit | Developer, central government, hydrologist | Hydrologist, climate risk, downstream users | Benefit real but climate/drought/operation limits unresolved |
| Ecological mitigation | Developer | Ecologist, NGO, downstream users | Unresolved; species-specific proof absent |
| Resettlement and sacred sites | Developer, local government conditional | Social expert, affected residents, legal expert | Approval blocker until livelihood and cultural process are completed |
| Engineering readiness | Developer | Dam safety, hydrologist, climate risk | Feasible concept but not construction-ready |
| Economic viability | Developer | Economist, financier, climate risk | Marginal; downside and full safeguards not modeled |
| Legal/safeguard readiness | Central government conditional | Legal expert, financier, residents, NGO | Not ready for final approval |

## Targeted Round 2 Questions

1. Hydrologist and Ecologist: can the 22% environmental flow rule be accepted now?
2. Economist and Climate Risk: what is the plausible combined downside BCR direction?
3. Upstream Residents and Developer: is redesign around sacred sites possible, or is this an absolute blocker?
4. Dam Safety: what activity, if any, can proceed before EAP, left-abutment investigation, and climate PMF update?
5. Financier: what happens if safeguard conditions are not met before construction?
6. Legal/Policy: what approval label is procedurally defensible now?

## Provisional Moderator Diagnosis

The defensible recommendation is likely **Redesign / Defer final build approval**, not **Build as proposed**. A narrow early-stage planning authorization may be possible, but construction approval should not be granted until the blocking conditions are resolved.
