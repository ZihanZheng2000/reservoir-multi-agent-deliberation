# Round 2 Targeted Deliberation: SRD-001-co-full

## Exchange 1: Environmental Flow and Endemic Fish

**Hydrologist:** The 22% minimum cannot be accepted as final because the scenario says it may be too low for fish spawning in two dry-season months (F2.10). Dry-season inflow is only 18% of annual inflow (F2.2), so a percentage rule could produce very low absolute releases during the exact months when spawning matters.

**Ecologist:** I agree. The issue is compounded: spawning habitat in the inundation zone is lost (F7.2, F7.3), and the downstream flow regime may also be insufficient (F2.10). Fish passage is proposed (F7.7) but questioned for the endemic species (F7.8). I cannot accept post-approval ecological study as sufficient.

**Developer:** We accept the need for a species-specific ecological-flow study and fish passage assessment. We ask that these be treated as redesign conditions rather than grounds for permanent rejection, because the reservoir also addresses water security and flood control (F2.4-F2.6, F3.2).

**Moderator status:** Unresolved. Ecological flow and fish mitigation are construction-blocking redesign conditions, not minor monitoring requirements.

## Exchange 2: Combined Downside Economics

**Climate Risk Expert:** The existing downside BCR is already 0.86 under high-cost and dry-climate assumptions (F5.3). Because climate-adjusted inflow is incomplete (F11.1), this should not be treated as a complete stress test. Drought reduces firm yield and hydropower (F2.7, F4.4), while sedimentation reduces storage over time (F2.8).

**Economist:** The P90 cost is USD 2.05B (F5.2), and safeguard costs are not finalized because livelihood restoration and biodiversity plans remain incomplete (F8.4, F9.6). A combined case of P90 cost, dry climate, sedimentation, and full safeguard cost would likely be below 0.86, but the exact number cannot be calculated from the packet. The defensible statement is that the economic case is marginal and under-modeled.

**Developer:** The base BCR remains above 1.0 at 1.18 (F5.3). Rejecting the project on an uncalculated combined scenario would overstate uncertainty.

**Moderator status:** Resolved in part. The base benefit is credible, but final approval requires an explicit combined stress scenario and full safeguard-cost inclusion.

## Exchange 3: Sacred Sites and Redesign

**Upstream Affected Residents:** We oppose the current design because two sacred river sites are in the inundation zone (F8.3), and livelihood restoration is not defined (F8.4). We are not saying every possible project is impossible. We require a genuine option to reduce reservoir height or change the footprint if that avoids sacred sites.

**Developer:** We cannot promise avoidance without engineering and yield analysis. We can commit to modeling a reduced reservoir level and reporting the effect on active storage, firm yield, hydropower, and cost.

**Social Expert:** This should be treated as a prerequisite to meaningful consultation. People cannot consent to a process where the only option is losing sacred sites with cash compensation.

**Moderator status:** Conditional opening. Redesign analysis around sacred sites is required before final approval.

## Exchange 4: Safety Boundary

**Dam Safety Engineer:** Preparatory desktop studies, non-invasive surveys, and limited work outside the dam footprint may proceed. No dam foundation excavation, diversion works, or structural construction should begin until left-abutment investigation, climate-adjusted spillway review, seismic loading analysis, and EAP are complete (F6.4-F6.6, F1.6).

**Central Government:** This distinction is useful. It allows the project to continue studies without confusing study authorization with construction approval.

**Moderator status:** Resolved. Study-stage authorization may proceed; construction approval should not.

## Exchange 5: Financing Condition

**Financier:** Development-bank financing is not ready because acceptable E&S management is required (F9.8), and safeguard gaps remain unresolved (F10.7). If construction proceeds without our 25% tranche, the project must cover about USD 355M at P50 or USD 512M at P90 from other sources (F5.2, F5.4; inference). This is a material financing risk.

**Economist:** This reinforces the need to include financing delay and replacement financing in the economic model.

**Moderator status:** Resolved. Financing readiness is a blocker for construction approval.

## Exchange 6: Procedural Approval Label

**Legal / Policy Expert:** The procedurally defensible label is not Build. It is either Redesign with final approval deferred, or Defer final approval pending EIA review, RAP completion, biodiversity offset plan, consultation validation, and lender safeguard clearance (F9.3, F9.4, F9.6, F9.8).

**Central Government:** Agreed. The project can remain in the pipeline, but the approval decision should be staged.

**Moderator status:** Resolved. Final build approval is premature.
