# Single-Agent Baseline: SRD-001-co-full

## Prompted Role

A single decision analyst reviewed the same neutral scenario packet and produced one integrated recommendation without multi-agent role separation or deliberation.

## Recommendation

**Defer final approval pending major redesign, safeguard completion, and additional studies.**

## Main Reasoning

The project has real public benefits: base-case firm yield exceeds the projected urban demand gap (F2.4, F2.5), irrigation reliability improves (F2.6), flood peaks are reduced for 1-in-20-year events (F3.2), and hydropower contributes flexible peaking power (F4.1-F4.3).

However, the approval package is not ready. The most important blockers are incomplete climate-adjusted hydrology (F11.1), marginal downside economics (F5.2, F5.3), incomplete EIA and safeguard plans (F9.3, F9.6), incomplete livelihood restoration and grievance mechanism (F8.4, F8.6), and high ecological uncertainty for endemic fish and spawning habitat (F7.2, F7.3, F7.8).

## Conditions

1. Complete independent EIA review, RAP, biodiversity offset plan, and consultation validation.
2. Complete climate-adjusted inflow, yield, drought, and PMF analysis.
3. Recalculate BCR with P90 cost, sedimentation, full safeguards, and climate stress.
4. Resolve endemic fish mitigation and environmental flow requirements.
5. Complete left-abutment, EAP, and seismic safety work.
6. Conduct a serious non-dam portfolio comparison.

## Limitation Relative to Multi-Agent

The single-agent baseline reaches a defensible direction, but it compresses stakeholder conflict. It does not separately preserve the developer's staged-approval argument, upstream residents' sacred-site condition, financier disbursement refusal, or the ecologist's stronger objection that mitigation must be proven before approval.
