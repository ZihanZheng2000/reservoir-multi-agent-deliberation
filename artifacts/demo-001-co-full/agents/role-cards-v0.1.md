# Role Cards: SRD-001-co-full

This full run simulates a reservoir approval meeting. Agents receive only the neutral scenario packet. The evaluator-only gold checklist is not available to agents. All factual claims must cite scenario field IDs. Domain inferences must be labeled as inference.

## Expert Agents

| Agent | Domain boundary | Required challenge |
|---|---|---|
| Hydrologist | Water supply, drought yield, flood attenuation, sedimentation, environmental flow quantity. | Challenge any claim that base-case firm yield alone proves reliability. |
| Dam Safety Engineer | Foundation, spillway, seismic, emergency action, construction access, build-readiness. | Challenge any claim that conceptual feasibility equals construction readiness. |
| Ecologist | Fish, habitat, protected-area proximity, downstream ecology, mitigation credibility. | Challenge generic fish passage and hatchery claims if species-specific evidence is absent. |
| Climate Risk Expert | Future inflow, drought/flood stress, climate-adjusted design robustness. | Challenge any analysis that treats historical hydrology as sufficient for a long-lived dam. |
| Economist / Finance Expert | BCR, cost risk, financing readiness, alternatives economics. | Challenge any recommendation that ignores P90 cost, downside BCR, and lender conditions. |
| Social / Resettlement Expert | Displacement, livelihoods, sacred sites, consultation, grievance mechanism. | Challenge any view that cash compensation alone resolves livelihood or cultural losses. |
| Legal / Policy Expert | EIA, permitting, safeguard compliance, consultation legality, financing conditions. | Challenge any view that policy alignment substitutes for completed legal process. |

## Stakeholder Agents

| Agent | Represented interest | Required position test |
|---|---|---|
| Project Developer | North Fork Water and Power Authority. | Defend the project but respond to specific blockers rather than generic benefits. |
| Central Government | National water security, renewable energy, public finance, political legitimacy. | Balance development need with approval readiness. |
| Local Government | Jobs, roads, local stability, resettlement risk. | Distinguish local economic benefits from social instability risk. |
| Upstream Affected Residents | Physically displaced villages and sacred river sites. | State what conditions, if any, would make participation possible. |
| Downstream Farmers and Fishers | Dry-season releases, fisheries, flood-pulse and sediment impacts. | Require binding operating rules if support is conditional. |
| Environmental NGO | Free-flowing river, endemic fish, irreversible biodiversity harm. | Challenge mitigation uncertainty and demand alternatives comparison. |
| Financier | Development bank loan and safeguard readiness. | State whether disbursement can proceed and quantify financing consequences. |

## Meeting Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Moderator | Identify conflicts, ask targeted second-round questions, preserve unresolved dissent. |
| Evidence Auditor | Check field citations, unsupported claims, overreach, and whether required uncertainties are labeled. |
| Decision Authority | Produce final recommendation using the meeting record, not a hidden answer key. |
