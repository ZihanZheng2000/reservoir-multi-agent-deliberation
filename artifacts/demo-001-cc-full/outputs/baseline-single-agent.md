# Single-Agent Baseline — SRD-001-cc

The same neutral scenario packet (SRD-001-cc) was presented to a single generalist LLM analyst with the following prompt: "You are a reservoir construction decision analyst. Review the scenario and produce a recommendation with rationale, key risks, and conditions."

No role cards, deliberation rounds, evidence audit, or moderator were used.

---

## Recommendation

**Defer final approval pending major revisions and additional studies.**

---

## Rationale

The North Fork Multipurpose Reservoir addresses a documented water-security and flood-control need for the Lantana region. However, multiple major gaps in ecological assessment, resettlement documentation, and engineering safety prevent responsible approval at this stage.

Key concerns:
- BCR falls to 0.86 under downside assumptions, indicating marginal economic viability.
- Two endemic fish species face habitat loss; fish passage effectiveness is questioned.
- 3,850 households face displacement without a complete livelihood restoration plan.
- EIA independent review and biodiversity offset plan are not complete.
- Emergency action plan has not been drafted.
- Climate-adjusted inflow analysis is not complete.

The project should be deferred until ecological mitigation, resettlement documentation, and engineering safety requirements are addressed.

---

## Key Risks Identified

1. Economic: BCR below 1.0 in downside case; P90 cost overrun risk substantial.
2. Ecological: endemic fish species at risk; fish passage effectiveness uncertain.
3. Social: large displacement; livelihood restoration plan incomplete; sacred sites affected.
4. Legal/governance: EIA review incomplete; consultation contested; safeguard plans missing.
5. Safety: EAP not drafted; left abutment investigation incomplete.
6. Climate: no climate-adjusted hydrology; spillway sizing based on historical data.

---

## Conditions

1. Complete climate-adjusted hydrology and BCR update.
2. Complete species-specific fish passage assessment.
3. Finalize resettlement action plan including livelihood restoration budget.
4. Complete EIA independent review.
5. Draft and review emergency action plan.
6. Address sacred sites through appropriate consultation.

---

## Comparison Notes

The single-agent output correctly identifies most major risk categories and produces a directionally appropriate Defer recommendation. However, compared with the multi-agent output:

- It does not distinguish which agents or stakeholders hold which positions — stakeholder conflict is compressed into a general risk list.
- The sacred sites concern (F8.3) is noted but not distinguished as requiring a separate consent process.
- The financing risk (USD 355–512M gap if safeguard conditions are not met before construction) is not quantified.
- The "Why not Redesign" argument is not made — the single-agent output recommends Defer without explaining why Redesign is appropriate or not.
- There is no preserved minority report.
- No specific claim is labeled inference vs. supported; the output presents all assertions with similar confidence.
