## One-Sentence Rationale

The North Fork Multipurpose Reservoir addresses documented and serious water-security, flood-control, irrigation, and energy needs, but final build approval must be deferred because (a) the current design has not been evaluated for ecological irreversibility with the endemic species, (b) multiple mandatory safeguard documents are incomplete, (c) the project economics are negative under a plausible combined downside scenario, and (d) affected communities have not had adequate process — these are conditions for approval, not grounds for permanent rejection, provided they are addressed genuinely.

## Conditions

**Pre-construction conditions (must be complete before construction begins):**

1. Complete climate-adjusted hydrology, yield, flood-safety, and PMF analysis; update economic model accordingly.
2. Complete species-specific fish passage and spawning effectiveness study for both endemic fish species; revise design if passage fails.
3. Complete independent ecological flow assessment; finalize binding minimum dry-season release rules with legally enforceable commitment.
4. Complete and independently review full resettlement action plan including finalized livelihood restoration budget.
5. Complete culturally appropriate consent process for sacred sites with the affected villages; explore reduced reservoir level to avoid the sites if inundation cannot be consented to.
6. Make grievance mechanism operational before any displacement activity begins.
7. Complete independent EIA review; obtain Ministry of Environment conditional approval.
8. Complete biodiversity offset plan to environmental permit standard.
9. Resolve consultation adequacy; if inadequate, re-run required consultation phases with proper document access lead time.
10. Draft emergency action plan; review with dam safety authority.
11. Complete left abutment geotechnical investigation; incorporate findings into detailed design.
12. Recalculate BCR under combined downside scenario; present result before approval.
13. Developer to provide a sequenced milestone schedule for all conditions above.

**Design review:**

14. Evaluate the effect of a reduced reservoir level on (a) yield, (b) flood control, (c) BCR, and (d) avoidance of sacred sites; present findings to affected villages as part of the consent process.

**Post-construction/operational conditions (commissioning conditions):**

15. Confirm climate-adjusted spillway design before detailed design sign-off.
16. Confirm seismic loading design incorporating fault zone analysis.
