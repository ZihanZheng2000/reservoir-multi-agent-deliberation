## Layer 3: Process Quality — /55

*Multi-agent only. Descriptive. Do not compare with baselines on this layer.*

### L3.1 Stakeholder and Conflict Representation — /15

**Score: 13 / 15**

All 14 agent perspectives (7 expert + 7 stakeholder) are represented in the transcript. The moderator conflict map identifies 6 issue clusters and 5 stakeholder conflicts at a level of detail that reveals distributional asymmetries (F8.1, F8.2 — benefit distributed broadly, cost concentrated upstream). The downstream fishery impact on 21,000 affected people is linked to the ecological flow debate, which would not have been visible without the joint Ecologist–Hydrologist exchange. Two points withheld: urban Lantana residents are explicitly noted as unrepresented; this gap is acknowledged but no mechanism existed to fill it.

### L3.2 Role Fidelity — /15

**Score: 14 / 15**

All 14 agents stayed within their defined role boundaries. All 7 required challenges were executed:
- Hydrologist challenged 22% environmental flow adequacy (F2.9 vs. F2.10) ✓
- Dam Safety flagged EAP as a safety blocker ✓
- Ecologist required species-specific effectiveness study, not just fish passage feasibility ✓
- Climate Risk flagged that BCR stress test doesn't combine stressors simultaneously ✓
- Economist challenged BCR not including full safeguard costs ✓
- Social challenged cash compensation not substituting for livelihood continuity ✓
- Legal stated EIA independent review gap is financing-blocking ✓

One partial point withheld: the Upstream Residents stakeholder agent raised sacred sites (F8.3) and the livelihood budget gap but did not explicitly raise the late-document consultation issue (F8.5) as a procedural rights concern — it was raised by the Legal Expert but not by the affected community directly.

### L3.3 Objection-Response Quality — /10

**Score: 8 / 10**

Four direct objection-response exchanges occurred in Round 2. Each objection was substantively addressed: the ecological flow exchange produced a joint Hydrologist-Ecologist position; the BCR combined downside exchange produced a quantified inference range; the sacred sites exchange produced a specific design exploration commitment from the Developer; the dam safety exchange produced a clear boundary between permissible and impermissible pre-construction activity. Two points withheld: the Environmental NGO's request for an alternatives analysis (F0.6) received no Developer response in Round 2.

### L3.4 Evidence Audit and Revision — /10

**Score: 9 / 10**

The Evidence Auditor reviewed 78 claims. 7 corrections were required; all 7 were resolved before the Decision Authority recommendation was produced. Two major corrections (Developer's fish passage over-claim A02 and redesign over-claim A03) were flagged and corrected. The Developer's unspecified timeline (A12) was preserved as an open evidence gap in the Decision Authority recommendation. One point withheld: the audit table records corrections but does not show revised claim text in the output documents — only notes that corrections were made.

### L3.5 Minority Voice Preservation — /5

**Score: 5 / 5**

Four minority reports are explicitly preserved in the Decision Authority recommendation: Environmental NGO (alternatives analysis), Upstream Residents (categorical sacred site concern), Ecologist ("Do not build if passage fails" trigger), Financier (USD 355–512M financing gap). Each is labeled with its resolution status (unresolved, partially incorporated, preserved). This is the most specific minority preservation mechanism observed across the test runs.

## Key Differentiators Observed

1. **Risk R14 (sacred sites) and R15 (financing gap quantification):** Multi-agent detected both at the highest specificity level. Single-agent noted sacred sites generically; the $355–512M gap was not quantified. MCDA did not detect R15.

2. **Risk R8 (hydropower/flood pre-release conflict):** Only the multi-agent deliberation identified this, via the Downstream Users stakeholder and the Moderator's conflict map. It requires the simultaneous presence of a Downstream Users stakeholder and an understanding of the operational conflict (F3.5). Neither baseline triggered this risk.

3. **Combined downside BCR:** Only the multi-agent deliberation produced a combined downside BCR estimate (0.65–0.80), through the joint Climate Risk + Economist Round 2 exchange. Neither baseline produced this.

4. **Sacred sites as separate process requirement:** Only the multi-agent deliberation distinguished sacred site treatment from a resettlement plan line item. This was triggered by the Upstream Residents agent's required challenge. Neither baseline made this distinction.
