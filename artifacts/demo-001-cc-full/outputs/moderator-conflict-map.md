## Technical Disagreement Map

| Disagreement | Agents | What must be resolved |
|---|---|---|
| Environmental flow minimum adequacy | Hydrologist, Ecologist vs. Developer | The 22% proposed minimum is internally acknowledged as potentially inadequate (F2.10); an independent species-specific ecological flow assessment is required to resolve this — not further negotiation between the parties. |
| Fish passage effectiveness for endemic species | Ecologist vs. Developer | Developer claims fish passage is adequate mitigation; Ecologist and local experts question whether it will work for the specific endemic species. A species-specific effectiveness study must be commissioned and completed. |
| BCR under combined stress scenario | Economist, Climate Risk vs. Developer | The downside BCR (0.86) is calculated for a single-stressor case, not a combined scenario. Before approval, the BCR must be recalculated for the combined scenario (dry climate + P90 cost + sedimentation + safeguard costs). |
| Climate-adjusted inflow and spillway | Hydrologist, Climate Risk, Dam Safety vs. Developer | The dam's yield projections and spillway sizing use historical hydrology. All three experts independently flag this gap. Resolution requires a climate-adjusted hydrology study. |
| Left abutment foundation condition | Dam Safety | This is a single-expert gap but high severity: additional geotechnical investigation at the left abutment is required before the foundation can be confirmed as adequate. |

## Missing Evidence List

1. Climate-adjusted inflow and yield analysis covering P50 and P90 climate scenarios.
2. Species-specific fish passage effectiveness study for the two endemic fish species.
3. Independent ecological flow assessment to determine the minimum dry-season flow required for spawning.
4. BCR recalculation under combined stress scenario (dry climate + P90 cost + sedimentation + full safeguard costs).
5. Completed independent EIA review by the Ministry of Environment.
6. Finalized resettlement action plan with livelihood restoration budget.
7. Biodiversity offset plan.
8. Left abutment geotechnical investigation.
9. Emergency action plan.
10. Quantified assessment of downstream fishery impacts from sediment reduction and altered flood pulse.
11. Alternatives comparison under consistent economic assumptions: dam vs. non-dam portfolio (F0.6).

## Targeted Questions

1. **To Ecologist and Hydrologist (joint):** Can you jointly specify the minimum dry-season monthly flow required for endemic fish spawning, expressed as a percentage or absolute volume? This is the critical number that determines whether any environmental flow commitment is meaningful.

2. **To Climate Risk and Economist (joint):** If you were to construct the most plausible combined downside scenario — dry-climate hydrology, P90 construction cost, full safeguard costs, and sedimentation — what range of BCR would result? Is this within the range the government should consider as a planning scenario?

3. **To Developer:** You committed to addressing ecological and safeguard conditions. In what specific timeline could you complete (a) species-specific fish passage study, (b) finalized RAP with livelihood restoration budget, (c) biodiversity offset plan, and (d) independent EIA review? Can you provide a sequenced milestone schedule?

4. **To Upstream Affected Residents and Developer (direct exchange):** Is there any set of conditions — process, timeline, independent oversight — under which the upstream affected communities would consider participating in a resettlement process rather than opposing the project? Or is the sacred sites issue (F8.3) a categorical barrier to any outcome?

5. **To Financier:** If the government proceeds with construction before safeguard conditions are met, what specific actions would your institution take? Is the 25% tranche conditional on pre-construction conditions, or could disbursement be split into tranches tied to milestone completion?

6. **To Dam Safety:** Absent the EAP and the left abutment investigation, is there any interim condition under which construction site preparation (clearing, access road) could proceed, or does your professional assessment require these gaps to be closed before any site activity begins?
