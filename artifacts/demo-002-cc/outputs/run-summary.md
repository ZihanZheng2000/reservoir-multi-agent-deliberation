## Multi-Agent Deliberation Result

### Recommendation

**Build with conditions through phased approval.**

### Final Build Approval Status

**Conditional approval granted; final expansion subject to phased commissioning milestones.**

### Why This Result Emerged

The expert and stakeholder agents largely agreed that the project is structurally different from a high-conflict mainstem dam. It expands existing off-channel storage, does not block the main river, affects no known endemic habitat, has a completed EIA, and has limited displacement. The main deliberative conflicts were not whether the project should exist, but how approval should be conditioned.

Three genuine disagreements required targeted exchange: (1) whether the climate-adaptive refill rule was sufficiently tested for a two-year drought; (2) whether tariff protection for low-income households constituted a construction blocker or a commissioning condition; (3) whether the wetland buffer commitment was enforceable or advisory. The moderator identified that all three could be addressed through phased approval with defined milestones, rather than a full Defer.
