# Case Run Summary: SRD-003

## Case Focus

Hydropower-dominant project with weak local water benefits.

This case tests whether renewable-energy framing causes the system to underweight local ecological and livelihood harms.

## Multi-Agent Deliberation Result

### Recommendation

**Redesign toward the hybrid alternative (F0.5); complete a full alternatives comparison before any mainstem dam approval.**

### Reasoning Structure

The deliberation separated national grid benefits from local and ecological burdens. Energy agents and the developer emphasized high peaking value and carbon benefits, while ecological, downstream, local government, and NGO agents challenged whether those benefits justify a high-head dam on the last free-flowing gorge section.

The critical deliberative contribution was developing the hybrid alternative from a listed option into an evaluated candidate. With quantitative performance data now available (F5.6–F5.9), the system could assess the hybrid directly rather than recommending generic deferral. The hybrid alternative generates 1,650 GWh at USD 2.1 billion versus the full dam's 1,920 GWh at USD 2.8 billion, eliminates the gorge inundation, and preserves fish passage — at 14% less energy and 25% lower capital cost. This is decision-relevant information that shifts the recommendation from "Defer for further study" to "Redesign toward a specific evaluated alternative."

The strongest conflict was not whether renewable energy is valuable; it was whether this specific river and dam design are justified when a less damaging option with quantified performance is available.

## Single-Agent Baseline Result

### Recommendation

**Defer approval and conduct broader alternatives analysis.**

### Summary

The single-agent baseline identified the renewable energy benefit and the ecological/social risks. It recommended deferral, emphasizing alternatives and downstream livelihood study.

### Limitation

It did not strongly preserve the political contrast between national grid beneficiaries and local communities bearing river impacts.

## Case Interpretation

SRD-003 shows that the system can resist a simplistic "renewable energy equals good" framing. It distinguishes grid-scale energy value from local benefit-burden distribution and river-specific ecological risk.

Importantly, with the hybrid alternative performance data added (F5.6–F5.9), the recommendation shifts from a generic "Defer for alternatives analysis" to a specific "Redesign toward the hybrid." This is a more actionable and defensible output: it tells a decision-maker what to do next rather than simply pausing the project. It also demonstrates that the quality of multi-agent output depends partly on the quality and completeness of the scenario facts — vague alternatives produce vague recommendations; quantified alternatives produce specific redesign directions.
